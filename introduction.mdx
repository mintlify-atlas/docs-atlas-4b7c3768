Incidencias Backend is a full-stack civic reporting platform built on Laravel 12. It provides a REST API for a mobile application (built with Ionic) that allows citizens to submit geo-located incident reports — such as potholes, broken streetlights, or public safety issues — and an admin web panel where government administrators can review, manage, and resolve those reports.

## What Incidencias Backend does

The platform connects two types of users:

- **Citizens** register via the mobile app, submit their identity documents (CURP + INE), and once approved by an admin, can report incidents at GPS coordinates with photos and descriptions.
- **Administrators** access a web panel to verify citizen accounts, review incident reports by category, update report statuses (pending → in process → resolved), and view heatmaps and statistics.

## How the platform works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Citizen registers">
    A citizen submits their name, email, password, CURP, and INE document photos through the mobile API. Their account starts in `pending` status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Admin reviews identity">
    An administrator logs into the web panel, reviews the submitted documents, and approves, requests corrections, or rejects the account. The citizen is notified by email.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Citizen reports an incident">
    Once approved, the citizen selects a category, adds a description and photo, and submits the report with their GPS coordinates.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Admin resolves the report">
    The admin views reports by category, updates the status to "in process" or "resolved," adds a comment, and optionally uploads evidence. The citizen receives a push notification and email.
  </Step>
</Steps>
